# Inference Over Intuition: A Portfolio-Based Assignment for Teaching Statistical Inference in Undergraduate Econometrics

This repository contains materials for the classroom assignment described in:

**"Inference Over Intuition: A Portfolio-Based Assignment for Teaching Statistical Inference in Undergraduate Econometrics"**

The assignment uses a stock portfolio simulation to help students understand the distinction between realized outcomes and population-level inference. Students construct two portfolios: one based on intuition and one based on quantitative indicators. They then analyze the outcomes using statistical inference to evaluate whether observed differences represent meaningful population-level differences.

The goal of the assignment is not to teach finance or investment strategy, but rather to provide a concrete setting for teaching:
- sampling variability,
- hypothesis testing,
- confidence intervals,
- correlation,
- interpretation of empirical results.

## Assignment Overview

Students work in teams to construct two hypothetical stock portfolios over a fixed investment horizon:

1. **Intuition-Based Portfolio**
   - Students select assets based on their own beliefs, narratives, or expectations about future performance.

2. **Indicator-Based Portfolio**
   - Students select assets using quantitative measures derived from historical return data.

After the investment period, students compare portfolio outcomes and conduct statistical analysis to evaluate whether differences in realized performance provide evidence of systematic differences between strategies.

## Learning Objectives

After completing the assignment, students should be able to:

1. Distinguish between observed sample outcomes and underlying population parameters.
2. Explain why realized differences may arise from sampling variability.
3. Conduct and interpret hypothesis tests comparing empirical outcomes.
4. Recognize the importance of uncertainty when drawing conclusions from data.
